<p><p>The Photonic Integrated Circuit (PIC) market consists of three primary types of integration: monolithic, hybrid, and module. Monolithic integration involves fabricating all optical components on a single substrate, optimizing performance and compactness. Hybrid integration combines separate optical and electronic components from different substrates to leverage the strengths of each material. Module integration focuses on assembling multiple integrated circuits into a single functional unit, facilitating easier deployment while maintaining efficient performance. Each type serves distinct applications and market needs.</p></p>
