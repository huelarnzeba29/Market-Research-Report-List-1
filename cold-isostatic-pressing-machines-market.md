<p><p>Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) machines are utilized to achieve uniform compaction of materials under high pressure, enhancing their densification. The market is segmented into two primary types: dry bag and wet bag. Dry bag CIP machines utilize a flexible pouch containing the powder, which is then compacted under pressure without any fluid medium. In contrast, wet bag CIP machines employ a liquid medium, typically water, in addition to the powder, allowing for better density control and enhanced material performance.</p></p>
