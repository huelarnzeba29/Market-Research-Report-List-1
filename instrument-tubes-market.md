<p><p>The instrument tubes market primarily consists of seamless and welded tubes. Seamless tubes are produced without any joints, offering superior strength and reliability, making them ideal for high-pressure applications. In contrast, welded tubes are created by fusing metal edges together, which allows for easier manufacturing and cost-effectiveness. These tubes are commonly used in lower-pressure applications. Both types serve critical roles in industries such as oil and gas, pharmaceuticals, and instrumentation, each catering to specific performance and budget requirements.</p></p>
