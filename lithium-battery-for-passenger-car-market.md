<p><p>The lithium battery market for passenger cars primarily consists of three types: NCM/NCA, LFP, and others. NCM (Nickel Cobalt Manganese) and NCA (Nickel Cobalt Aluminum) batteries are known for their high energy density and performance, making them suitable for long-range electric vehicles. LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) batteries emphasize safety and longevity, often found in lower-cost EV models. The “others” category includes various chemistries and technologies that cater to different performance, cost, and safety requirements in the automotive sector.</p></p>
