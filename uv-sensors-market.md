<p><p>UV sensors are specialized devices that detect different wavelengths of ultraviolet light, categorized into three main types: UVA, UVB, and UVC. UVA sensors measure longer wavelengths (320-400 nm) primarily impacting skin aging and tanning, while UVB sensors (280-320 nm) signify sunburn and skin cancer risks. UVC sensors (100-280 nm) focus on germicidal applications, as UVC light effectively kills microorganisms. Collectively, these sensors find applications in various sectors, including healthcare, environmental monitoring, and consumer products, enhancing safety and monitoring efficacy.</p></p>
